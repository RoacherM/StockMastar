# Wyckoff Phases

## Phase A: Preliminary Movement

Marks the end of prior trend.

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **PS** | Preliminary Support/Supply - First sign of trend exhaustion |
| **SC** | Selling Climax (or BC - Buying Climax) - High-volume climax |
| **AR** | Automatic Rally/Reaction - Initial countermove |
| **ST** | Secondary Test - Tests SC/BC on reduced volume |

## Phase B: The Cause

Accumulation or distribution occurs here. Price consolidates in a trading range (TR).

**Key characteristics:**
- Wide range bars with high volume (professionals absorbing supply/demand)
- Narrow range bars with low volume (quiet accumulation)
- Multiple tests of TR boundaries
- Final shakeout (Spring) or upthrust (UTAD)

## Phase C: The Test

Smart money tests if TR is ready to move.

| Event | Accumulation | Distribution |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| **Spring** | Price drops below TR on low volume, then recovers | - |
| **UTAD** | - | Price rises above TR on low volume, then falls |
| **LPS** | Last Point of Support - Bids absorb selling | Last Point of Supply - Offers cap rallies |

## Phase D: The Effect

Trend begins. Confirms with Sign of Strength/Weakness.

| Event | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **SOS** | Sign of Strength - Breakout with volume |
| **SOW** | Sign of Weakness - Breakdown with volume |
| **LPSY** | Last Point of Supply/Support - Retest of breakout |

## Phase E: The Markup/Markdown

Trend in motion. No new TR formed.

**Characteristics:**
- Strong directional movement
- Pullbacks find support/resistance at TR boundaries
- Volume expands on breakouts, contracts on pullbacks
